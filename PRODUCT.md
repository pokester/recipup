# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Dog owners across the UK and Netherlands who want to cook fresh food for their dogs but need the nutrition science handled for them. Three motivations sit equally at the table: the devoted dog parent who wants to know every ingredient; the cost-conscious owner who has compared fresh delivery prices and wants the same quality for less; and the health-anxious owner whose dog has specific conditions requiring precision nutrition.

Their context when they open the app: they care deeply about their dog, they've opted out of the convenience of delivery services, and they are about to cook something themselves. They are engaged, not passive.

## Product Purpose

Recipup generates personalised, nutritionally-complete recipes for dogs based on breed, age, weight, and health conditions. It handles the hard science, including FEDIAF and AAFCO compliance, breed-specific calorie targets, and health condition rules, so that users can focus entirely on cooking. The app also manages meal planning, pantry tracking, cost comparisons against fresh food delivery services, and weekly health check-ins.

Success looks like an owner who builds a recipe plan in a few minutes, cooks it that weekend, and logs a healthy check-in the following week. The product earns trust through accuracy, not through hype.

## Brand Personality

Calm, caring, expert. Not a wellness brand making vague promises. Not a clinical health portal. A knowledgeable friend who happens to know veterinary nutrition, who speaks plainly, and who genuinely cares about the dog at the end of the recipe.

Voice: warm but precise. Never preachy. Never overstated. Avoids phrases like "revolutionary" or "transform your dog's life". Instead: "We've done the research. You do the cooking."

## Anti-references

- Sterile health-tech: cold white backgrounds, clinical blues, chart-heavy dashboards, hospital portal aesthetic. Recipup is a kitchen tool, not a medical device.
- Pet industry kitsch: cartoonish paw prints, bubble fonts, primary colours, anything that signals "accessories store" over "serious nutrition".
- Generic SaaS: purple gradient heroes, identical stat-card grids, every B2B analytics tool from 2022.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence first, simplicity second, pride third.** Users should feel certain the recipe is right for their dog before they feel anything else. Ease of use is second. The satisfaction of having cooked it themselves is the lasting reward the product builds toward.
2. **Complexity belongs to us.** Breed calculations, FEDIAF standards, health condition rules, calorie maths: none of this should be visible or legible to the user. The product absorbs the hard work; the interface shows only what the cook needs to know.
3. **Warm kitchen, not cold clinic.** Every surface should feel like it belongs in a home with a dog in it, not in a health software company. Warmth is not decoration; it is the correct emotional register for a product that is fundamentally about care and food.
4. **Earn trust, don't claim it.** Trust comes from precision, not from reassuring copy. If the recipe says 142g of chicken breast, it means 142g. Labels, error messages, and empty states should be direct and specific, not vague and hedged.
5. **Progress accumulates.** The health log, the cost tracking, the recipe library: these should feel like an owner building something over time for their dog, not just using a tool. The product should reflect the history a user has built inside it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigability for all core flows. Sufficient contrast across the warm palette. Support for prefers-reduced-motion where animations are used.
