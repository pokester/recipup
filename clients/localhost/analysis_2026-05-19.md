# Website Analysis: Recipup (localhost:3000)

**Score: 6/10** — Strong value proposition and clear identity, but missing the signals that turn curious visitors into sign-ups.

---

### What's Costing You Customers

- **When someone shares your link on WhatsApp or Instagram, nothing shows up.** Dog food is a word-of-mouth product — parents tell other parents. Right now, a shared link produces a blank preview with just the URL. Adding a single recipe preview image as a social card could meaningfully increase referral traffic at zero ad spend.

- **"How it works" looks unfinished to a first-time visitor.** The three large numbered boxes are mostly empty space with tiny text underneath. A visitor who's never heard of Recipup sees what looks like a placeholder design and instinctively doubts the product's polish — before they've even read what it does.

- **Google can't connect you to the searches that matter.** Your page title is "Recipup" and nothing else. Someone searching "homemade dog food recipes UK" or "personalised dog food plan for my dog" won't find you — your competitors with keyword-rich titles get the click instead.

---

### What We'd Fix (in priority order)

1. **Add social sharing preview (OG tags + preview image)** — title, description, and a compelling image so every shared link shows a recipe card or the dog/kitchen visual. · _Quick win_
2. **Rewrite the page title** — from "Recipup" to something like "Recipup — Personalised Home-Cooked Dog Food Recipes" to capture keyword searches. · _Quick win_
3. **Redesign the "How it works" steps** — replace the large empty numbered boxes with something that shows the product in action: a mini form preview for step 1, a macro chart for step 2, a finished meal for step 3. · _Small project_
4. **Add Schema.org markup** — mark up the page as a SoftwareApplication so Google shows star ratings, pricing, and categories alongside your search result. · _Quick win_
5. **Sharpen the founding member pitch** — "Be part of something real" is good, but adding a counter ("47 of 500 spots claimed") or a named testimonial from an early member makes the urgency concrete rather than implied. · _Quick win_

---

### What Caught Our Eye

- **The cost comparison section earns its place.** Showing three numbered boxes — Fresh delivery ~£96/month, Standard delivery ~£60/month, Home cooking £32–45/month — with the home cooking box highlighted in coral puts the answer to "is this worth it?" on the page before a visitor even has to ask. That's exactly where most competitors lose the sale.

- **The founding member section uses real urgency.** "Be part of something real" with a named founding price avoids the fake-countdown-timer pattern that erodes trust everywhere else. A visitor who reads it understands the product is early and the price will go up — without being manipulated.

- **The trust bar below the hero is doing serious work quietly.** "Recipes to FEDIAF/AAFCO standards · Breed specific for 40+ breeds · Puppies, adults & seniors · Health conditions supported" reads like the credentials a veterinary nutrition brand would put on packaging. A dog owner scanning quickly gets "this is backed by real standards" without reading a single sentence of body copy.
